GSTIN

Know More

What & Why

The Goods and Services Tax Identification Number (GSTIN) is a mandatory registration for businesses in India. It is used to collect taxes, claim input tax credit (ITC), and file GST returns, unifying several indirect taxes into a single system.

Who Needs to Register?

Registration is mandatory for businesses whose annual turnover exceeds the threshold limit (e.g., ₹40 lakh for goods, ₹20 lakh for services). It is also required for e-commerce operators, businesses making inter-state sales, and others as specified by the GST law.

General Documents Required

Identity & Address: PAN and Aadhaar card of the proprietor/directors.
Business Proof: Certificate of Incorporation or Partnership Deed.
Location Proof: Electricity bill or rental agreement for the place of business.
Bank Details: A cancelled cheque or bank statement.

How to Apply for GSTIN Online

1. Visit GST Portal: Go to the official GST website (gst.gov.in).
2. Get TRN: Start a new registration by providing PAN, email, and mobile to generate a Temporary Reference Number (TRN).
3. Fill Application: Use the TRN to log in, fill out the detailed application form, and upload scanned copies of the required documents.
4. Submit & Verify: Submit the application using a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) or E-Signature (Aadhaar OTP).
5. Get ARN: An Application Reference Number (ARN) is generated for tracking. The GSTIN and certificate are typically issued within 3-7 working days after successful verification.

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

Know More

What & Why

TDS is a system where the entity making a payment (like an employer paying a salary or a bank paying interest) deducts a certain percentage as tax before giving the full amount to the recipient. This amount is deposited directly with the government, ensuring a steady tax collection and preventing evasion.

How It Works

The payer deducts the tax and provides the recipient with a TDS certificate (Form 16/16A). When you file your ITR, you can claim credit for this tax that has already been paid on your behalf. If the TDS paid is more than your total tax liability, you receive a refund.

Common Payments Subject to TDS

Salary: Deducted by the employer based on your income tax slab.
Interest: On bank deposits, bonds, and other investments.
Rent: For payments exceeding a certain annual threshold.
Commission & Brokerage: Fees for professional or technical services.

How to Check Your TDS

All tax deducted against your PAN is reflected in your Form 26AS (Annual Tax Statement). You can view and download this form for free from the official Income Tax e-Filing portal to verify your TDS credits before filing your ITR.

Income Proof

Know More

What & Why

Income proof refers to any official document that certifies an individual's or business's income. It is essential for securing loans, renting property, visa applications, and as a general measure of financial credibility.

Common Documents for Salaried Individuals

Latest Salary Slips: Typically, the last 3 to 6 months.
Form 16: The TDS certificate issued annually by your employer.
Bank Statement: A 6-month statement showing regular salary credits.
ITR-V: The acknowledgement received after filing your Income Tax Return.

Common Documents for Self-Employed & Businesses

Income Tax Returns (ITR): Usually required for the last 2-3 financial years.
Audited Financials: Profit & Loss Statement and Balance Sheet.
Bank Account Statement: Both business and personal, for at least the last 6 months.
Form 26AS: Your annual consolidated tax statement from the IT portal.

How to Obtain

Salary slips and Form 16 are provided by your employer. Bank statements are available from your bank. ITR documents and Form 26AS can be downloaded from the official Income Tax e-Filing portal.

MSME Registration

Know More

What & Why

MSME stands for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises. The official registration process is called Udyam Registration. It provides businesses with benefits like priority sector bank loans, access to government schemes, subsidies, and preference in government tenders.

MSME Classification Criteria

Enterprises are classified based on a composite criteria of Investment and Annual Turnover:
Micro: Investment up to ₹1 Crore & Turnover up to ₹5 Crore.
Small: Investment up to ₹10 Crore & Turnover up to ₹50 Crore.
Medium: Investment up to ₹50 Crore & Turnover up to ₹250 Crore.

How to Register Online

The entire process is free, paperless, and based on self-declaration.
1. Visit Portal: Go to the official Udyam Registration portal.
2. Enter Details: Use the Aadhaar number of the proprietor, partner, or director to start the process.
3. Auto-Fetch Info: PAN and GST-linked details on investment and turnover are fetched automatically.
4. Submit & Receive: Upon submission, a permanent Udyam Registration Number (URN) is generated and an e-certificate is issued.

Key Information

No documents need to be uploaded for registration. The process is entirely free of cost and no private agencies are required.
