Identity Documents

Aadhaar Card

A 12-digit unique ID for Indian residents, proof of identity and address.

What & Why

Issued by UIDAI, it's a voluntary ID for every resident of India.

Used for: Identity/Address proof, opening bank accounts, filing ITR, getting a new SIM, and availing government welfare schemes.

Eligibility

Any individual, regardless of age and gender, who is a resident of India. NRIs are not eligible.

Documents for New Enrollment

Proof of Identity (PoI): PAN Card, Passport, Driving License.
Proof of Address (PoA): Voter ID, Bank Statement, Electricity/Water Bill (last 3 months).
Proof of Date of Birth (DoB): Birth Certificate, SSLC Certificate.

How to Get a New Aadhaar Card

1. Visit Centre: Locate and visit a nearby Aadhaar Enrolment Centre.
2. Form & Docs: Fill the enrolment form & submit original copies of PoI & PoA.
3. Biometrics: Provide fingerprints, iris scan, and a photograph.
4. Track: Use the Enrolment ID (EID) on the acknowledgement slip to track status online via the UIDAI portal.

Updatable Information

You can update demographic data (Name, Address, DoB, Gender, Mobile Number, Email) and biometric data (Fingerprints, Iris, Photograph) at an enrolment centre.

PAN Card

A 10-character alphanumeric ID, essential for all financial transactions and tax purposes in India.

What & Why

The Permanent Account Number (PAN) is a unique 10-digit code issued by the Income Tax Department. It is mandatory for filing tax returns, high-value transactions, opening bank/demat accounts, and serves as a valid proof of identity.

Eligibility

All Indian citizens, including minors, are eligible. Foreign citizens and entities (like companies) that conduct financial transactions in India can also apply.

Documents for Application (Indian Citizen)

Identity Proof: Aadhaar Card, Voter ID Card, Driving License.
Address Proof: Aadhaar Card, recent Utility Bill, Bank Statement.
Date of Birth Proof: Birth Certificate, Matriculation Certificate, Aadhaar Card.

How to Apply for a New PAN Online

1. Visit Portal: Go to the official portals of Protean (formerly NSDL e-Gov) or UTIITSL.
2. Fill Form 49A: Select "New PAN" and fill out Form 49A with accurate details.
3. Upload & Pay: Upload scanned copies of your photo, signature, and required documents. Pay the processing fee online (approx. â‚¹107).
4. Await Delivery: After successful verification, the e-PAN is delivered to your email ID in a few hours/days, and the physical card is mailed to your address.

Correction or Reprint

To make changes to your existing PAN or request a reprint, use the 'Changes or Correction in existing PAN Data' form on the same portals.

Voter ID Card

An identity document issued by the ECI, enabling citizens to cast their vote in elections.

What & Why

The Indian Voter ID Card or EPIC (Electors Photo Identity Card) is issued by the Election Commission of India (ECI). It confirms a citizen's eligibility to vote and also serves as a general proof of identity and address.

Eligibility

You must be an Indian citizen, at least 18 years old on the qualifying date, and an ordinary resident of the polling area where you wish to enroll.

Documents for New Registration

Proof of Identity: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Driving License, Passport.
Proof of Address: Aadhaar Card, Rental Agreement, latest Utility Bill.
Photograph: One recent passport-sized color photograph.

How to Apply for a New Voter ID Online

1. Visit Portal: Go to the official Voters' Service Portal (voters.eci.gov.in).
2. Register & Login: Create an account and log in with your credentials.
3. Fill Form 6: Select and fill out 'Form 6' for new registration.
4. Upload & Submit: Upload scanned copies of your photo and documents. Submit the form to get a reference ID for tracking.
5. Verification: A Booth Level Officer (BLO) may visit your address for verification before the ID is approved and issued.

Other Services

The portal can also be used to check your name in the electoral roll, apply for corrections (Form 8), track application status, and download the digital Voter ID (e-EPIC).

Indian Passport

The official government document for international travel and a powerful proof of identity and nationality.

What & Why

An Indian Passport is issued by the Ministry of External Affairs, allowing citizens to travel internationally. It is a globally accepted document serving as the ultimate proof of Indian citizenship, identity, and address.

Eligibility & Types

All citizens of India are eligible.
Types: Ordinary (Blue), Official (White), and Diplomatic (Maroon).

Documents for New Application (Adult)

Proof of Date of Birth (DoB): Birth Certificate, Aadhaar Card, PAN Card.
Proof of Address: Aadhaar Card, Voter ID Card, latest Utility Bill, Bank Passbook.
Non-ECR Proof (if applicable): Class 10 certificate or higher educational documents.

How to Apply for a New Passport Online

1. Register Online: Create an account on the Passport Seva portal.
2. Fill Application: Log in and fill out the fresh passport application form.
3. Pay & Schedule: Pay the fee and book an appointment slot at a nearby Passport Seva Kendra (PSK).
4. Visit PSK: Attend the appointment with all original documents for verification, biometrics, and photograph.
5. Delivery: After successful police verification, the passport is printed and sent to your address via Speed Post.

Tatkaal Scheme

For urgent requirements, the Tatkaal scheme offers faster processing for an additional fee, subject to specific conditions and documentation.

Visa

Official permission granted by a country to enter, stay, or work for a specific period.

What & Why

A visa is a conditional authorization granted by a country to a foreigner, allowing them to enter, remain within, or leave that country. It is typically a stamp or sticker affixed to the applicant's passport.

Common Visa Types

Tourist: For leisure, travel, or visiting friends/family.
Student: For pursuing education in a foreign institution.
Work/Employment: For taking up a job in another country.
Business: For commercial activities, meetings, or negotiations.
Transit: For passing through a country to a third destination.

General Required Documents

This varies by country, but common requirements include: A valid passport (min. 6 months validity), completed application form, passport-sized photos, proof of financial means, travel itinerary (flight/hotel bookings), and a cover letter stating the purpose of travel.

General Application Process

1. Check Embassy Website: Visit the official embassy/consulate website of your destination country for the latest requirements.
2. Fill Application & Gather Docs: Complete the visa application form and collect all necessary documents.
3. Book Appointment: Pay the fee and schedule an appointment at a Visa Application Centre (e.g., VFS Global).
4. Attend Appointment: Submit your documents and provide biometrics (fingerprints/photo).
5. Track: Use the provided tracking number to monitor your application's status online.

Driving License (DL)

An official document permitting you to operate a motor vehicle on public roads in India.

What & Why

A Driving License, issued by the Regional Transport Office (RTO), is mandatory for driving a motor vehicle in India. It also serves as a common proof of identity and address across the country.

Types & Eligibility

Learner's License (LL): The first step, required for learning to drive.
Permanent License (DL): Full license issued after passing the driving test.
Eligibility Age: 16+ for gearless two-wheelers; 18+ for geared two-wheelers (MCWG) and cars (LMV).

Documents for Learner's License

Common documents include: Proof of age (Birth/School Certificate), Proof of address (Aadhaar/Voter ID), application Form 1, Medical Certificate (Form 1A), and passport-sized photos.

How to Apply for a License Online

1. Visit Parivahan Portal: Go to the official Sarathi Parivahan website.
2. Apply for LL: Fill the Learner's License application, upload documents, and pay the fee.
3. Pass LL Test: Book a slot and clear the online test on traffic rules and signs.
4. Apply for DL: After 30 days (and within 6 months) of getting your LL, apply online for the permanent DL.
5. Pass Driving Test: Book a slot and visit the RTO to pass the practical driving test.
6. Receive DL: The permanent Driving License is dispatched to your registered address after you pass the test.

Birth Certificate

The first official identity document of an individual, recording the event of their birth.

What & Why

A Birth Certificate is a vital record that documents the birth of a child. It is essential for school admissions, obtaining other documents like passports and voter IDs, and for legal proof of age and identity.

Registration Timeline & Location

Timeline: It is mandatory to register every birth with the concerned local authorities within 21 days.
Location: In hospitals (by the institution), Municipal Corporation offices (urban areas), or Gram Panchayat offices (rural areas).

How to Register (Within 21 Days)

The head of the household must inform the local registrar by filling out the prescribed form (Form-1). The hospital where the birth occurred usually provides proof. This process is free of charge and the certificate is issued after basic verification.

Delayed Registration

After 21 days to 30 days: Registration is allowed with a prescribed late fee.
After 30 days to 1 year: Requires written permission of the Registrar and a late fee.
After 1 year: Can only be registered with an order from a First Class Magistrate and a late fee.

Online Services

Many states, through the central Civil Registration System (CRS) portal, allow for online registration of births (if reported within 21 days) and searching for old records.

Death Certificate

An official document certifying the death of a person, issued by the local authorities.

What & Why

A Death Certificate is a legal document recording the cause, date, and place of a person's death. It is crucial for settling property inheritance, claiming insurance benefits, and relieving the deceased from social and legal obligations.

Registration Timeline

It is legally mandatory to register every death with the local registrar within 21 days of its occurrence. The process is managed by the Civil Registration System (CRS).

Who Can Register

The head of the household is responsible for reporting the death. If the death occurs in a hospital or institution, the designated authority from that institution is responsible for reporting it to the registrar.

Required Information

Key details needed are the deceased's age, gender, date and time of death, cause of death (medical certificate), and identity/address proof of the deceased and the applicant.

Delayed Registration

After 21 days to 30 days: Registration is allowed with a prescribed late fee.
After 30 days to 1 year: Requires written permission of the concerned Registrar.
After 1 year: Can only be registered based on an order from a First Class Magistrate.

Marriage Certificate

The official legal document that establishes the marital status of a couple.

What & Why

A Marriage Certificate is a legal proof of marriage. It is essential for changing maiden names, applying for spouse visas, joint bank accounts, and claiming life insurance or inheritance.

Governing Laws in India

Hindu Marriage Act, 1955: For Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, or Sikhs where the marriage is already solemnized.
Special Marriage Act, 1954: For all citizens irrespective of religion, especially for inter-faith marriages.

General Documents Required

Application form, Address & DoB proof for both partners (Aadhaar, Birth Certificate), passport-sized photos, wedding invitation card, affidavits, and ID proof for 2-3 witnesses.

Application Process

Under Hindu Act: Apply at the Sub-Registrar's office in the jurisdiction where the marriage was performed. The certificate is typically issued after verification of documents.
Under Special Act: Give a 30-day notice of intended marriage to the Marriage Officer. If no objections are received, the marriage is solemnized and the certificate is issued.

Online Services

Many state governments now provide online facilities to fill the initial application and book an appointment at the Registrar's office, streamlining the process.

Caste Certificate

An official statement certifying that a person belongs to a particular caste, tribe or community.

What & Why

A Caste Certificate is proof of an individual's belonging to a reserved category like Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), or Other Backward Class (OBC). It is essential for availing benefits like reservation in government jobs, admission to educational institutions, and various welfare schemes.

Issuing Authority

This certificate is issued by the respective state governments through authorities like the Tehsildar, Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM), or District Collector's office.

General Documents Required

An application form, proof of identity & address (Aadhaar, Voter ID), an affidavit, and most importantly, proof of caste of a blood relative (like father or uncle), or other supporting documents as per state rules. An income certificate is often required for OBC candidates.

How to Apply

The process varies by state.
1. Online: Many states have their own e-District or service portals where you can apply, upload documents, and track status online.
2. Offline: You can apply by submitting the filled application form and required documents at the local Tehsildar's office or a Common Service Centre (CSC).

Verification

After submission, a verification process is typically conducted by local revenue officials, which may include a field inquiry, before the certificate is approved and issued.

Educational Certificates

Official documents like marksheets and degrees certifying academic qualifications.

What & Why

These are documents (marksheets, pass certificates, degrees) issued by an educational institution. They are crucial proof of your academic qualifications, required for higher education admissions, employment, and visa applications.

Key Certificates in India

School Level: Class 10th & 12th Marksheets and Pass Certificates.
Higher Education: Bachelor's Degree, Master's Degree, Diploma Certificates, and yearly/semester marksheets.

Issuing Authorities

Certificates are issued by recognized School Boards (like CBSE, CISCE, State Boards) and Universities (as per UGC guidelines) across India.

How to Access Digitally via DigiLocker

DigiLocker is a Government of India initiative to access authentic digital documents.
1. Sign Up: Create a DigiLocker account using your Aadhaar number.
2. Browse Partners: Go to the Education category and select your board or university.
3. Fetch Documents: Enter your details (Roll No., Year, etc.) to fetch your digital certificate directly from the source.
4. Use Digitally: These digital certificates are legally valid and on par with physical originals.

Ration Card

A government document enabling households to buy subsidized food grains from the Public Distribution System.

What & Why

Issued by state governments, a Ration Card allows eligible households to purchase essential food items like wheat, rice, and sugar at subsidized rates from Fair Price Shops (FPS) under the National Food Security Act (NFSA). It also acts as a common proof of address and identity.

Types of Ration Cards

Based on a household's economic status, cards are generally categorized as:
AAY (Antyodaya Anna Yojana): For the poorest families, offering the highest subsidy.
PHH (Priority Household): For families meeting state eligibility criteria, entitled to a fixed quantity of grains per member.
APL (Above Poverty Line): For households with higher incomes, who may receive items at less subsidized rates or not at all.

General Documents Required

Application form, Aadhaar cards of all family members, proof of address (utility bill), income certificate, and passport-sized photographs of the head of the family.

Application Process

Applications are managed by the Food, Civil Supplies & Consumer Affairs department of each state. The process can typically be initiated online through the state's specific food portal or offline at the local Circle/Taluk office.

One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC)

This scheme allows beneficiaries to access their entitled food grains from any Fair Price Shop in the country, which is especially useful for migrant workers.

Pension Card (PPO)

An identifier, primarily the PPO number, required for receiving monthly pension benefits.

What It Is

In India, a "Pension Card" is most often the Pension Payment Order (PPO) number. This is a unique 12-digit number given to each pensioner under the Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS) by the EPFO, or a similar identifier for government pensioners.

Why It's Important

The PPO number is a crucial reference for all communication with the pension-sanctioning authority. It is required to receive the monthly pension, transfer the pension account between banks, and most importantly, for submitting the annual Life Certificate (Jeevan Pramaan).

Who Issues It

EPFO: For employees of the private sector covered under the EPS-95 scheme.
Central/State Governments: For retired government employees.

How to Get Your PPO

The PPO is generated by the concerned authority (like EPFO) after processing the pension claim upon retirement. The number is communicated to the pensioner and the bank branch where the pension is to be credited.

Jeevan Pramaan (Life Certificate)

Pensioners must submit a Life Certificate annually to continue receiving their pension. This can be done digitally using the PPO number at various centres or through the Jeevan Pramaan portal, proving that the pensioner is still alive.

State-Specific Cards

Cards issued by the Karnataka Govt. to provide residents access to welfare schemes and benefits.

What & Why

State governments issue various cards to deliver targeted benefits like health insurance, subsidies, and financial aid to eligible residents. These supplement national IDs like Aadhaar.

Key Cards in Karnataka

Arogya Karnataka Card (AB-ArK): A health insurance card providing cashless medical treatment at empanelled hospitals for eligible families.
Labour Card (Karmika Card): Provides social security and welfare benefits like pensions, scholarships, and financial aid to registered construction workers.
Kutumba Card: A unique Family ID for every family in the state, designed to proactively deliver government benefits without needing separate applications.

How to Apply

Applications for most schemes are processed through the Seva Sindhu portal, which is Karnataka's one-stop-shop for citizen services. Applicants can also visit physical centres like BangaloreOne, KarnatakaOne, or GramaOne.

General Documents Required

While specific documents vary by scheme, common requirements include Aadhaar Card, Ration Card, proof of residence, income certificate, and caste certificate (if applicable).

Life Insurance

A contract that provides financial protection to your family in the event of your demise.

What & Why

Life insurance is a contract with an insurer who, in exchange for regular premium payments, provides a lump-sum payment (Sum Assured) to your beneficiaries upon your death. It ensures your family can manage debts, meet goals, and maintain their lifestyle.

Common Policy Types

Term Insurance: Pure risk cover. Offers a large sum assured for a low premium for a fixed term, with no maturity benefit.
Endowment Plan: A combination of insurance and savings. Pays out on death or on policy maturity.
ULIP (Unit Linked Plan): Combines insurance with market-linked investments.
Money-Back Policy: Periodically pays out a portion of the sum assured during the policy term.

Key Factors to Consider

Cover Amount (Sum Assured): Should be at least 10-15 times your annual income.
Policy Term: Should cover you at least until your retirement age.
Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR): A high CSR (above 95%) indicates the insurer's reliability in paying claims.
Riders: Optional add-ons like critical illness or accidental death benefits for enhanced protection.

Tax Benefits (Income Tax Act, 1961)

Premiums paid are typically eligible for deduction under Section 80C. The death/maturity benefit is usually tax-free under Section 10(10D), subject to conditions.
