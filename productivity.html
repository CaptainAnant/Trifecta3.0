LinkedIn

The world's largest professional network for career development and connecting with industry professionals.

What is LinkedIn?

LinkedIn is a social networking platform designed specifically for the business community. Its goal is to help professionals connect, share, and learn, allowing users to build and maintain a network of professional contacts, find jobs, and stay updated on industry trends.

Key Features

Professional Profile: Acts as your digital resume, showcasing your skills, experience, and education.
Networking: Connect with colleagues, recruiters, and industry leaders to expand your professional circle.
Job Board: A comprehensive platform to search, discover, and apply for jobs globally and locally.
Content Feed: Share and consume professional content, articles, and news relevant to your industry.

LinkedIn Premium (Career)

Who can see you: See who has viewed your profile in the last 90 days.
InMail Credits: Directly message recruiters and hiring managers (even if you're not connected).
Applicant Insights: See how you compare to other applicants for a job.
LinkedIn Learning: Get access to thousands of expert-led online courses to learn new skills.

GitHub

A platform for hosting and collaborating on code using Git version control.

What is GitHub?

GitHub is a web-based platform built around the Git version control system. It's a central place for developers to store their code, manage projects, and collaborate with others from anywhere in the world. It is widely considered an essential tool in modern software development.

Key Features

Repositories: Centralized hosting for your Git version control projects.
Collaboration: Use Pull Requests, code reviews, and issue tracking to work with teams.
GitHub Actions: Automate your software development workflows, from building and testing to deploying.
GitHub Pages: Host simple, static websites directly from your repository for free.
GitHub Copilot: An AI pair programmer that suggests code and entire functions in real-time.

Plans & Pricing

Free: A powerful free tier for individuals and organizations with unlimited public and private repositories, 2,000 CI/CD minutes/month, and community support.
Team: Starts at ~$4 USD/user/month. Adds features like protected branches and more CI/CD minutes for professional teams.
Enterprise: Starts at ~$21 USD/user/month. Provides advanced security, compliance, and deployment controls for large organizations.
Copilot (Add-on): Available for individuals (~$10 USD/month) and businesses (~$19 USD/user/month) as a separate subscription.

Who is it for?

Essential for software developers, open-source communities, students learning to code, and any team that needs to collaborate on a software project. It's also used by writers and researchers for versioning documents.

Pinterest

A visual discovery engine for finding ideas like recipes, home and style inspiration, and more.

What is Pinterest?

Pinterest is a social media platform designed as a visual discovery engine, often described as a "catalogue of ideas." Users can discover, save, and share images and videos (called 'Pins') related to their interests, hobbies, and inspirations by organizing them into virtual 'Boards'.

Key Features

Visual Discovery: The core of Pinterest is its powerful visual search. Users can find ideas for everything from recipes and DIY projects to fashion and travel.
Pins & Boards: Pins are individual ideas (images or videos) that users save. Boards are collections of Pins, used to organize ideas by theme or project.
Pinterest Lens: Allows users to take a photo of an object in the real world to find similar items, styles, or ideas on the platform.
Shopping: Many Pins are shoppable, allowing users to click through and purchase products directly from a retailer's website.

For Businesses

Pinterest is a powerful tool for businesses, especially in e-commerce, lifestyle, and creative sectors. It's free to create a business account, which provides access to analytics and the ability to run ads. Businesses can use 'Promoted Pins' (ads) to reach a targeted audience based on interests and search behavior, driving traffic and sales.

Pricing

Pinterest is free for personal use. Businesses can use the platform for free but have the option to pay for advertising. Ad costs are budget-based, operating on a Cost-Per-Click (CPC) or Cost-Per-Impression (CPM) model, making it flexible for various marketing budgets.

Discord is a VoIP and instant messaging platform designed for creating communities. Users communicate with voice calls, video calls, text messaging, media, and files in private chats or as part of communities called "servers."

Reddit

A network of communities where people can dive into their interests, hobbies, and passions.

What is Reddit?

Reddit is a social news aggregation and discussion website. Content is organized into user-created communities called "subreddits," which cover virtually any topic imaginable. Users submit posts (links, text, images) that are then voted up or down by other members.

Core Features

Subreddits: Thousands of communities (e.g., r/technology, r/explainlikeimfive) for focused discussions.
Voting System: Upvotes and downvotes determine the visibility of posts and comments, ensuring popular content rises to the top.
Karma: A score that reflects a user's contributions to the community through posts and comments.
User Feeds: Create custom feeds by subscribing to various subreddits to tailor your browsing experience.

OBS Studio

A powerful, free, and open-source software for live streaming and video recording.

What is OBS Studio?

Open Broadcaster Software (OBS) Studio is the go-to free and open-source application for video recording and live streaming. It allows you to capture and mix video and audio from multiple sources in real-time to create professional broadcasts.

Core Features

Scene Composition: Create scenes with multiple sources like webcams, screen captures, images, text, and browser windows.
Audio Mixer: Intuitive audio mixer with per-source filters like noise suppression, noise gate, and gain control.
Streaming Integration: Natively supports all major streaming platforms including Twitch, YouTube, and Facebook Live.
Extensibility: Highly customizable with a vast library of third-party plugins and scripts to add new functionality.

Pricing & Versions

Completely Free: OBS Studio is 100% free to use for both personal and commercial purposes. There are no fees, subscriptions, or paid "pro" versions of the core software.
Open-Source: It's a community-driven project maintained by volunteer contributors from around the world.

Who is it for?

Ideal for content creators, gamers, educators, musicians, and businesses who need a robust and flexible tool for live streaming, creating video tutorials, recording gameplay, or hosting virtual events without any cost.
