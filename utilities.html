Utility & General Services

Bharat Gas Connection

One of India's leading providers of LPG for domestic and commercial use.

What & Why

A Bharat Gas connection provides a household with Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) for cooking. It is a subsidiary of Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited (BPCL) and is essential for receiving subsidized cylinders.

Documents for New Connection

Proof of Identity (PoI): Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID.
Proof of Address (PoA): Aadhaar Card, recent Utility Bill, Rent Agreement.
Other: Passport-sized photos and an affidavit stating no other LPG connection is held by the household.

How to Apply for a New Connection Online

1. Register Online: Visit the ebharatgas portal and fill out the 'Register for New Connection' form.
2. Complete KYC: Upload your scanned documents to complete the Know Your Customer (KYC) process.
3. Pay Online: Pay the security deposit for the cylinder and regulator.
4. Distributor Verification: Your application is sent to the nearest distributor for verification.
5. Receive Equipment: Once approved, the distributor issues the connection and delivers the cylinder and regulator.

Booking a Refill

Refill cylinders can be booked easily through various methods: the official portal/app, IVRS phone call, SMS, or via WhatsApp booking services.

Indane Gas Connection

Get a new domestic LPG connection from IndianOil for your household cooking needs.

What & Why

Indane is the official LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas) brand from Indian Oil Corporation (IOCL), a major public-sector provider in India. A domestic gas connection is essential for cooking in most households.

Documents for a New Connection (KYC)

Proof of Identity (any one): Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID Card, Passport.
Proof of Address (any one): Aadhaar Card, recent Utility Bill, Lease Agreement, Bank Passbook.

How to Apply for a New Connection

Online Method:
1. Visit the official IndianOil portal (cx.indianoil.in).
2. Register for an account and choose "Apply for New Connection".
3. Fill out the online application (KYC) form and upload scanned documents.
4. After verification, pay the security deposit for the cylinder and regulator online.
5. Your selected distributor will then process the request and deliver the equipment.

Offline Method:
Visit the nearest Indane distributor, fill out the application form, submit physical copies of your KYC documents, and pay the required deposit.

Other Online Services

The portal also allows you to book cylinder refills, make online payments, check your refill history, and request a second cylinder or other services.

HP Gas Connection

Get a new domestic LPG connection from Hindustan Petroleum for your household cooking needs.

What & Why

HP Gas is a major supplier of Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) in India. A registered domestic connection is required to procure LPG cylinders for cooking and ensures access to government subsidies and safety standards.

Connection Types

The most common domestic connections are for a 14.2 kg cylinder (standard for families) or a 5 kg cylinder (often called 'Chhotu', suitable for smaller households or temporary needs).

Documents for New Connection (KYC)

Proof of Identity (PoI): Aadhaar Card, Voter ID Card, Passport.
Proof of Address (PoA): Aadhaar Card, latest Utility Bill, Lease Agreement.
An affidavit declaring that your household does not already hold an LPG connection from any provider is also required.

How to Apply for a New Connection Online

1. Visit Portal: Go to the official My HP Gas website.
2. Register: Click on 'Register for New Connection' and complete the initial registration.
3. Fill Form: Enter your personal details, address, and select your nearest HP Gas distributor.
4. Upload & Pay: Upload scanned copies of your KYC documents and pay the security deposit for the cylinder and regulator online.
5. Verification & Delivery: After successful verification, the distributor will complete the process and deliver the equipment.

Offline Application

You can also visit your nearest HP Gas distributorship with the required documents to apply for a new connection in person.

Bangalore Water Supply (BWSSB)

Services for new connections, bill payments, and grievance redressal for water supply in Bengaluru.

Service Provider

The Bangalore Water Supply and Sewerage Board (BWSSB) is the government agency responsible for water supply and sewage disposal in the Bengaluru metropolitan area. The primary water source is the Cauvery River.

Key Online Services

Bill Payment: Pay monthly water bills online via the official portal.
New Connection: Apply for new domestic or commercial water connections.
Grievance Redressal: Register complaints regarding billing, water leaks, or supply issues.
Rainwater Harvesting: Access information and guidelines on RWH, which is mandatory for sites of certain dimensions in the city.

How to Pay Your Bill Online

1. Visit Website: Go to the official BWSSB portal.
2. Find Payment Link: Navigate to the 'Pay Your Bill' or 'Online Services' section.
3. Enter Details: Input your unique RR Number found on your physical water bill.
4. Make Payment: View your outstanding amount and pay using Net Banking, UPI, or Debit/Credit Cards.

Helpline & Support

For immediate assistance or to register a complaint, you can contact the 24/7 BWSSB helpline at 1916.

BESCOM Electricity

Official portal for Bangalore Electricity Supply Company Limited (BESCOM) services.

About BESCOM

BESCOM is responsible for power distribution in eight districts of Karnataka, including the Bangalore Urban area. It manages everything from new connections and billing to maintenance and customer support.

Key Online Services

Bill Payment: View and pay your monthly electricity bills online.
New Connection: Apply for a new electricity connection for your home or business.
Service Requests: Register for name changes, tariff changes, or load modifications.
Complaint Registration: Report power outages, billing issues, or other concerns.

How to Pay Your Bill

You can pay your bill online using your unique Account ID (found on your bill).
Online: Through the official BESCOM portal, or via apps like GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, etc.
Offline: At BESCOM counters, Karnataka One / Bangalore One centers, or designated payment kiosks.

Customer Support

For power outages or emergencies, you can contact the 24/7 BESCOM helpline at 1912. For other queries, you can use the support section on their official website.

Municipal Corp (BBMP)

Civic services by Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike for the citizens of Bengaluru.

What is BBMP?

The Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike is the administrative body responsible for all civic and infrastructural assets and services for the Greater Bengaluru metropolitan area.

Key Citizen Services

Property Tax: Online portal for assessment and payment of annual property taxes.
Khata Services: Registration, transfer, and bifurcation of Khata, the legal document for property assessment.
Birth & Death Certificates: Issuance and management of birth and death records within city limits.
Trade Licenses: Application and renewal of licenses required for businesses to operate in Bengaluru.
Building Plan Sanction: Approval of architectural plans for new constructions and modifications.

Infrastructure & Utilities

BBMP is also responsible for the maintenance of roads, storm-water drains, parks, and streetlights, as well as managing the city's solid waste collection and disposal.

Grievance Redressal

Citizens can report civic issues like potholes, garbage, or streetlight problems and track their complaints through the official BBMP website or the 'Sahaaya' online portal.

BDA (Bangalore Development Authority)

The principal planning authority for Bengaluru, responsible for urban development and infrastructure.

What is BDA?

The Bangalore Development Authority is an autonomous body responsible for the planned, long-term urban development of the Bengaluru metropolitan region. It focuses on creating new layouts and major infrastructure, while the BBMP (Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike) handles day-to-day civic administration.

Key Services & Responsibilities

Urban Planning: Preparing and implementing Master Plans for the city's growth.
Layout Formation: Developing new residential and commercial layouts with all necessary amenities.
Site Allotment: Allotting plots, homes, and apartments to the public.
Infrastructure Projects: Constructing flyovers, underpasses, and ring roads to improve connectivity.

Common Citizen Interactions

Citizens typically interact with the BDA for services such as:
- Applying for new sites or flats in BDA-formed layouts.
- Paying property tax for properties still under BDA administration.
- Obtaining building plan approvals and Khata certificates/transfers for BDA properties.

Online Portal

The official BDA website provides access to online payment gateways, application forms, allotment details, circulars, and status updates for various services.
